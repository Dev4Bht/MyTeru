# DrukSave — User Personas

## 1. Tashi Dema — Government Employee (Thimphu)

- **Age:** 29 · **Income:** Fixed monthly government salary
- **Goals:** Save consistently despite recurring family support obligations;
  build an emergency fund.
- **Pain points:** Salary disappears within two weeks; no visibility into
  where it went; feels guilty spending on herself.
- **Tech literacy:** Comfortable with smartphone apps, moderate data
  connectivity at work, good at home.
- **What she needs from DrukSave:** Automatic salary detection, a clear
  weekly "safe to spend" number, gentle nudges rather than guilt-inducing
  numbers.

## 2. Karma Wangdi — Taxi Driver (Paro)

- **Age:** 34 · **Income:** Daily cash income, highly variable
- **Goals:** Understand true daily profit after fuel/maintenance; save
  toward a motorcycle service fund and his children's education.
- **Pain points:** Income and expenses both happen in cash, in small
  amounts, throughout the day; no time to sit and do bookkeeping.
- **Tech literacy:** Basic smartphone use, prefers voice or one-tap entry
  over typing, intermittent connectivity between routes.
- **What he needs from DrukSave:** Fast expense capture (voice entry,
  minimal typing), offline mode, simple daily/weekly summaries.

## 3. Pema Choden — Fresh Graduate (Thimphu)

- **Age:** 23 · **Income:** First corporate salary, entry-level
- **Goals:** Learn how to budget for the first time; avoid overspending on
  dining and entertainment; start a "dream laptop" savings goal.
- **Pain points:** Never managed money independently before; social pressure
  to spend with new colleagues; doesn't know what "healthy" spending looks
  like.
- **Tech literacy:** High — digital native, expects a polished, Duolingo-like
  experience.
- **What she needs from DrukSave:** Educational nudges, goal-based savings
  with visual progress, a Financial Health Score she can improve like a game.

## 4. Sonam Tobgay — Farmer (Punakha)

- **Age:** 45 · **Income:** Seasonal agricultural income, large lump sums at
  harvest, sparse in between
- **Goals:** Smooth seasonal income across the year; plan for Losar and
  Tshechu expenses in advance; save for building material for a home
  renovation.
- **Pain points:** Income timing doesn't match expense timing; big cultural
  and family obligations arrive predictably but feel unplanned.
- **Tech literacy:** Lower — basic phone literacy, may prefer Dzongkha where
  available, needs large touch targets and simple language.
- **What he needs from DrukSave:** Seasonal cash-flow forecasting, festival
  spending planning, very simple UI.

## 5. Aum Deki — Retiree (Wangdue)

- **Age:** 63 · **Income:** Pension, fixed and modest
- **Goals:** Make a fixed pension last the full month; track medical and
  religious-offering spending; leave a little for grandchildren.
- **Pain points:** Fixed income with rising costs; anxious about running out
  of money before month-end; unfamiliar with financial apps generally.
- **Tech literacy:** Low — needs the simplest possible experience, large
  text, minimal steps, ideally family member can help set up.
- **What she needs from DrukSave:** Extremely simple dashboard answering
  "how much can I spend today," large readable numbers, no jargon.
